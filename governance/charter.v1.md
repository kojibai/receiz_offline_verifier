## Purpose

This charter defines governance authority, decision rights, and accountability for deterministic verification and settlement standards operated by Receiz.

## Control Owners

| Role | Responsibility Scope | Decision Rights |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Issuer (`Receiz Governance Office`) | Publishes standards, policy documents, and control boundaries. | May draft and propose revisions. |
| Approval Authority (`Receiz Core Protocol Review`) | Approves normative and high-risk standard changes. | May approve, reject, or require remediation before release. |
| Risk Owner (`Enterprise Risk and Resilience`) | Owns residual risk acceptance, exception approvals, and corrective action closure. | May accept/reject risk exceptions and enforce mitigation deadlines. |

## Authority Principles

1. Deterministic verification claims SHALL map to code and evidence artifacts.
2. Deterministic settlement claims SHALL map to replayable ledger math and persisted tuple fields.
3. No release SHALL bypass conformance controls without a recorded exception and risk-owner approval.
4. High-risk changes SHALL enforce separation of duties between author and approver.
5. Incident and exception records SHALL be retained as immutable governance evidence.
