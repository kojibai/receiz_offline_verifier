# Receiz v112.0.0 Product Truth

`@receiz/sdk` v112.0.0 preserves **first admission only, then append forever**.

## Frozen Primitives

1. Exact verified sealed bytes establish artifact truth.
2. Admission establishes profile membership, not operation authorization.
3. Verified identity admission establishes actor evidence.
4. Verified history establishes predecessor state.
5. The portable transition digest identifies successor meaning without idempotency or external effects.
6. The operation plan binds the transition to actor, resource, canonical registry, registry-derived effects, idempotency identity, and named commit domain.
7. A verified capability authorizes that exact plan within actor, tenant, operation, resource, registry, effects, Kai validity, algorithm, key, and revocation bounds.
8. The seal proves candidate bytes; neutral staging durably preserves those exact bytes.
9. Atomic acceptance independently resolves and verifies staged bytes before advancing one named domain head.
10. The receipt reports the accepted artifact, transition, actor evidence, history, effects, and head without becoming authority.

Namespace preservation is mandatory. Unknown namespaces survive byte projection and transition. Known namespace changes require registry-authorized reducers. Committed history comes from the independently accepted artifact, not from a plan, caller report, receipt, or cache.

## Frozen Invariants

- Artifact verification and profile admission remain separate from operation authorization.
- Verified actor evidence and verified history are runtime-custodied inputs; structural lookalikes cannot replace them.
- Portable transition, operation plan, capability, staged candidate, commit command, named domain, and receipt identities remain distinct.
- Effects are derived only from the pinned current registry and cannot be caller-expanded.
- Neutral staging advances no accepted head, and commit independently resolves and verifies the exact staged bytes.
- Named-domain head, event, receipt, effects, idempotency, and outbox changes are atomic or absent.
- Durable local admission truth stores no proof object or sealed artifact bytes, so known artifact truth paints first.
- A receipt is reporting evidence and cannot be fed back as actor, artifact, history, capability, plan, or commit authority.

## Bounded client rails

The browser admission ledger is durable local admission truth, not cache. It coordinates aggregate head, events, receipt, effects, idempotency, and outbox atomically while storing neither proof objects nor sealed artifact bytes. Known artifact truth paints first. `remoteStore` and `reconcileOffline` are deliberately absent because remote global admission and offline-to-global reconciliation are outside v112.0.

No product HTTP route or database migration was added. MCP remains coordination beneath `@receiz/sdk` and sealed artifact truth.
