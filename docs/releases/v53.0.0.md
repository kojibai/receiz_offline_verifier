# Receiz v53.0.0

Release date: April 14, 2026

## Overview

`v53.0.0` is the release that ties together the full market lineage that started in `v52.0.0`, was hardened in `v52.5.0`, and now extends into a cleaner public product system.

Since `v52.0.0`, Receiz has moved market from a compelling surface into a governed execution layer: deterministic quote math, replayable quote evidence, partial certificate custody, dedicated market conformance, premium runtime hardening, public market share routes, and market-aware playback. Since `v52.5.0`, the repository has continued with persisted market conformance history, public market JSON feeds, stronger prediction finalization logic, passkey account-attachment flows, first-class Enterprise entitlement handling, a simpler beginner-first front door, and a public-key-based release attestation model.

The core rule still does not change. The file remains authoritative for verification. Deterministic manifests remain authoritative for route truth. Ledger-backed settlement remains authoritative for payment truth. Market remains an execution layer around those truths, not a replacement for them.

The product-truth freeze for this release is defined in `docs/releases/v53.0.0-product-truth.md`.

## What Defines v53.0.0

- The full market system from `v52.0.0` and `v52.5.0` is now carried forward with public market asset data routes, indexed market surfaces, persisted conformance history, and stronger prediction resolution rules.
- Market desk, value-pill, and player surfaces now behave better on touch devices, expose richer chart context, and open more cleanly into viewer and playback flows.
- Public proof and market surfaces now emit stronger machine-readable metadata, structured data, sitemap coverage, and Open Graph treatment instead of stopping at human-facing pages only.
- Existing email accounts now connect Face ID / passkeys from signed-in account surfaces, while the public passkey route correctly refuses to silently enroll an already-claimed email.
- Enterprise entitlements are now first-class in shared plan/session/world access logic instead of being treated like an unsupported edge case.
- Public onboarding has been simplified around Verify, Seal, Record, and Public Twin. World is now framed as the advanced expansion layer, not the first concept a new user needs to learn.
- `/login` now behaves as a developer login-embed surface and no longer competes with the normal user sign-in path.
- Release attestations now use `Ed25519` signatures verified from the published governance keyring, with legacy HMAC verification retained only for old artifacts.
- Release-governed version surfaces now move together to `v53.0.0`.

## Since v52.0.0

### 1. Market Stayed Governed Instead Of Regressing Into A UI Feature

`v52.0.0` made market execution deterministic. `v52.5.0` hardened that model into a more coherent premium system. `v53.0.0` keeps that entire line intact.

The market still resolves through:

- one canonical quote engine
- spread as quote behavior instead of a visible bolt-on fee
- replayable quote evidence
- partial share-certificate custody
- dedicated market conformance
- lighter pill and desk runtime behavior

That foundation is not being replaced in this release. It is being extended outward into public routes, historical evidence, and cleaner user entry points.

### 2. Market Became Public, Indexable, And Machine-Readable

The market is no longer only a live desk for signed-in operators.

This release adds or hardens:

- `/market` as an indexable public route
- `/market/assets/[receizId]` as an indexable public market-asset route
- `/api/market/public-snapshot` as a cacheable public market JSON feed
- `/api/market/assets/[receizId]` as a cacheable machine-readable market asset record
- richer market Open Graph images and asset-specific dataset metadata
- sitemap coverage for market asset pages and public live-player pages
- proof and market metadata helpers for structured keywords and machine meta

This matters because the market now presents itself as a real public surface with crawlable and shareable truth, not just as an internal desk state.

### 3. Market Desk And Value Pill Became More Tactile

The market desk and lightweight pill surfaces now behave better in product reality.

The repository state prepared for `v53.0.0` carries:

- a touch-aware `ResponsiveTapButton` so pill actions do not misfire on touch and pen input
- compact chart tooltips with pulse and UTC context
- optional chart overlay tooling for range, trend, and fib views
- tighter backside metric cards and cleaner position/action rows
- a direct viewer entry from market surfaces when a live artifact experience exists
- market playback hydration routes so public assets can open cleanly into the live player
- player surfaces that now carry the market value pill inline
- global player updates that preserve dock theme and viewer hydration hints when market playback refreshes

This is not a cosmetic-only pass. It reduces input friction, improves legibility, and keeps the user closer to the trade, proof, and media state in one system.

### 4. Market Conformance Became Historical, Not Just Live

Earlier market releases gave the platform a live conformance report. `v53.0.0` turns that into a history system.

The release now persists conformance snapshots and daily rollups through:

- `public.market_conformance_snapshots`
- `public.market_conformance_daily_rollups`
- `app/lib/marketConformanceHistory.ts`
- `GET /api/market/conformance/history`
- `GET /api/market/conformance/history/rollups`

The live conformance route now records snapshot IDs, recorded timestamps, and attestation state instead of returning a disposable one-off report only.

That change matters because governance needs more than “what is true right now.” It needs historical evidence that can survive beyond one request.

### 5. Prediction Resolution Got More Precise And Less Ambiguous

Prediction markets have been tightened in two important ways.

First, resolution now works from micro-dollar precision rather than rounded cents when comparing the reference price and final price. That prevents false ties or flips near the margin.

Second, close/finalize logic is now stricter:

- wagers reject closed or expired contracts more reliably
- expiry can resolve from pulse or timestamp rather than one brittle path
- forced finalization is formalized in SQL
- ambiguous finalize paths now have a dedicated migration and resolution logic instead of relying on looser book-state behavior

This is the kind of hardening that matters after a market exists. The edge case is the product.

### 6. Public Proof Surfaces Got Stronger Search And Metadata Discipline

The proof system now emits more complete metadata rather than limiting itself to visible page text.

This release adds:

- proof metadata keyword builders
- proof machine-readable meta builders
- derived media-hash fallback behavior in proof manifests
- structured-data coverage on public live-player pages when the shared surface is public
- stronger SEO contract tests around proof, player, market, and sitemap surfaces

The user-facing result is that public proof pages, live players, and market asset pages are more self-describing to crawlers, cards, and machine consumers.

### 7. Passkey Attachment Now Follows Real Account Ownership

The passkey work in this release closes an important identity gap.

Before this change, public passkey registration could drift too close to acting like an account-claim path for an already existing email user. `v53.0.0` corrects that:

- existing email accounts cannot silently enroll from the public `/api/auth/passkey/begin` register path
- those accounts now get an explicit `passkey_enroll_from_account` error
- signed-in users can open a dedicated `/api/auth/passkey/enroll/begin` flow from Account or profile-owner surfaces
- staged email claims remain pending until the passkey ceremony actually succeeds
- the end-to-end auth test now covers these cases

That keeps passkeys aligned with account continuity instead of letting enrollment semantics weaken identity ownership.

### 8. Enterprise And The Public Ladder Are Now Coherent

Receiz had already described a public ladder that included Enterprise, but the shared plan logic was still incomplete.

This release makes Enterprise behave as a first-class entitlement in shared code:

- `app/lib/premium/plan.ts`
- `app/lib/premium/getPlan.ts`
- `app/lib/world/access.ts`
- `app/lib/world/types.ts`
- `app/lib/world/twinMindTransport.ts`
- `app/components/receipt/ReceiptCard.tsx`

Enterprise now:

- keeps its own plan rank
- inherits business and automation access where appropriate
- remains stable when session plan and entitlement plan differ
- no longer falls through lower-plan logic in world access or receipt append gating

That closes a mismatch between public product language and actual runtime behavior.

### 9. The Public Front Door Got Simpler After The Market Expansion

Market got deeper. The public explanation had to get simpler.

The current repository state repositions the public entry points around four concrete actions:

- Verify
- Seal
- Record
- Public Twin

And it deliberately pushes more advanced concepts back:

- World is now explained as an advanced expansion layer after the front door is working
- `/receiz-world` now redirects into the Public Twin world section instead of continuing as its own canonical explainer
- `/login` is now a developer embed surface and is marked `noindex`
- start, seal, make, home, and what-is-a-receiz surfaces reduce jargon and collapse advanced options until needed
- Business is now framed as the most common next upgrade, while Automation is positioned after Business is already live

This is a product-discipline release as much as a feature release. It protects comprehension after the system became more capable.

### 10. Release Attestation Moved To Public-Key Verification

Release governance in this repository now steps up from secret-only verification to public-key verification.

`v53.0.0` changes release attestation generation and verification so that:

- new artifacts are signed with `Ed25519`
- the signing key ID must match an active key in `public/governance/governance-keyring.v1.json`
- verification is reproducible from the published keyring and schema
- legacy `HMAC-SHA256` artifacts still verify only as a backward-compatibility path

This is one of the most important non-UI changes in the release. It makes release proof more legible to third parties and aligns it with the platform’s broader public-governance posture.

## Public Summary

Receiz `v53.0.0` carries forward the full market system introduced in `v52.0.0` and hardened in `v52.5.0`, then extends it into public market data routes, historical conformance evidence, stronger prediction resolution, touch-safe market interactions, cleaner proof and player metadata, proper passkey account attachment, first-class Enterprise handling, simpler beginner-first product surfaces, and publicly verifiable `Ed25519` release attestations.

## Deployment Notes

This release expects the earlier market migrations from the `v52.0.0` and `v52.5.0` market line to already be present, plus these new migrations:

- `supabase/migrations/20260414004000_market_prediction_finalize_ambiguity_fix.sql`
- `supabase/migrations/20260414012000_market_conformance_history.sql`

Before tagging `v53.0.0`, production should also be checked for:

- `GET /api/market/public-snapshot` returning the public market feed
- `GET /api/market/assets/[receizId]` returning a public machine-readable asset record for a public asset
- `GET /api/market/conformance/history` and `/api/market/conformance/history/rollups` hydrating after the live conformance route runs
- prediction contracts rejecting new wagers after expiry and resolving borderline price comparisons correctly
- signed-in account surfaces being able to connect Face ID / passkeys for an existing email user
- `/login` remaining a noindex developer-login surface while `/signin` remains the normal user path
- `/receiz-world` redirecting to `/public-twin#world`
- public version badges, service worker version, and offline verifier surfaces all showing `v53.0.0`

Release attestation now additionally requires:

- `RELEASE_ATTESTATION_SIGNING_KEY` to contain an `Ed25519` PKCS#8 private key PEM or base64-encoded PEM
- `RELEASE_ATTESTATION_SIGNING_KEY_ID` to match an active published key in `public/governance/governance-keyring.v1.json`
