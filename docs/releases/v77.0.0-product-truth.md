# Receiz v77.0.0 Product Truth

Release date: May 8, 2026
Status: Locked for `v77.0.0` release prep

## Purpose

This document defines the product-truth freeze for Receiz `v77.0.0`, the Signal Circuit, Creator OS, Vault Settlement, and Runtime Trust release after the `v76.0.0` candidate.

`v77.0.0` preserves the `v70.0.0` public alpha law, `v74.0.0` Receiz Key / identity-artwork baseline, `v75.0.0` local-proof/file-restore law, and `v76.0.0` World / Market / Public Proof law while adding portable verified Signal Cards, account-scoped Signal Vault continuity, durable creator production queues, sealed vault restore, public conformance assurance, and runtime-stale recovery discipline.

For this offline verifier repository, the release is represented as current-release documentation, canonical release artifacts, shipped HTML version markers, and package-metadata alignment only. The verifier proof-format and producer-payload contract remain unchanged.

## Core Product Truth

`v77.0.0` carries forward `v70.0.0` public alpha law, `v74.0.0` identity baseline, `v75.0.0` local-continuity law, and `v76.0.0` public proof / market law:

> Identity is local. Proof is in the file. Verified truth is append-only. `/<username>` is canonical profile truth. Settlement and Reserve preserve funded causality. The network propagates and appends; it does not get to downgrade stronger proof already held by the device.

## Locked Release Surface

### 1. Signal Circuit Is a Real Product Surface

- `/game` is a dedicated Signal Circuit route, not a loader shell or reused placeholder.
- Mobile game chrome, deterministic local game logic, and issue paths are part of the locked surface.
- Signal Card issuance uses existing Receiz proof primitives.

### 2. Signal Cards Are Portable Verified Collectibles

- Signal Cards carry sealed claims, deterministic rarity, card kind, character/utility mechanics, battle profiles, lineage, visual profile, and standard verification paths.
- Achievement truth and transferable collectible truth stay separated.
- Card artwork is generated, stored, replayed, and exported as part of the card contract.
- Purchased and recovered cards must not downgrade to older cached art or weaker partial card payloads.
- Downloaded verified card fronts use `☤KAI` in steward, valuation, and related visible copy instead of old Pulse framing.

### 3. Signal Packs and Vault Settlement Are Durable

- Signal Pack purchases are wallet-aware, exact-rate, resumable, and settlement-safe.
- A pack opening is incomplete until sealed cards are issued, stored, and recoverable from the durable opening record.
- Signal Vault is account-scoped, paginated, retryable, and protected from account bleed.
- Vault flows preserve card truth across navigation and support ownership transfer, listing, market rows, and audit rows without a one-card bottleneck.

### 4. Card Public, Market, Share, and Battle Surfaces Render From Sealed Truth

- Card public pages, share pages, OpenGraph/Twitter images, proof panels, listings, auctions, reserve-first purchase flows, and battle discovery render from sealed card truth.
- Liquid loader and placeholder replacement paths are out of bounds for these surfaces.
- Signal Arena battle history is append-only.
- AI twin defense requires owner opt-in.
- Battle results and progression records never mutate the original card claim.

### 5. Signal Pack Foundry Preserves Primitive Isolation

- Brand pack campaigns may define tiers, visible odds, supply caps, pack limits, arena/AR options, manifest seals, campaign records, and verification isolation.
- Signal Pack Foundry must not redefine or weaken the existing Signal Card primitive.

### 6. Signal Circuit Conformance Is Public Assurance

- Requirements, claim-to-code checks, source digests, scarcity proof, bounded simulation, badges, history, rollups, and status inventory move together.
- Public/developer Status includes Signal Circuit and high-value public card/conformance endpoints.
- Private vault, pack purchase, admin, webhook, worker, and legacy keyring surfaces stay out of the public trust surface.

### 7. Creator Workspace and Creator OS Are Durable Production Surfaces

- `/creator` is a first-class route, not a reuse of `/make`.
- Creator first paint uses settled local workspace truth.
- Streamdown is used for creator output.
- Private AI, wallet, assets, publish jobs, and launch jobs stay behind explicit action paths.
- Creator OS includes durable project, brand, run, message, event, output, asset, version, usage-ledger, publish-job, and safety-review storage.
- Signed-in API boundaries and worker-drain authorization are required.

### 8. Creator Paid Production Preserves Wallet Truth

- AI runs, asset lanes, and managed publishing debit wallet value idempotently before background production continues.
- Shortfalls open embedded wallet funding.
- Managed launch jobs can queue GitHub and Vercel work, use Vercel Sandbox when enabled, preserve provider proof, and record durable launch output without moving users away from Receiz.

### 9. Vault Restore and Document Sealing Are Stronger

- Verified Data Vault downloads linked bytes before sealing.
- Vault exports verifiable ZIP/vault packages, restores original files, and opens image/audio/video/PDF/native previews from restored bytes.
- Old Safari document sealing no longer falls back to legacy V3.
- When native Ed25519 is unavailable online, old Safari uses Signature V4 document sealing.
- Offline old Safari tells users to upgrade Safari 17+ or go online.

### 10. Runtime Recovery and Public Hydration Stay Honest

- Runtime stale recovery no longer mounts an automatic root interstitial or redirects from the stale guard.
- Legacy recovery assets silently clear stale runtime state and return to a stable path.
- Market share quote bootstrap batches long asset lists before Supabase `in` filters.
- Wallet, Account, global chrome, bottom chrome, game chrome, Creator, Vault, Status, Developers, Payments, Public Proof, Seal, and verified player surfaces may evolve only without weakening canonical profile truth or local proof continuity.

### 11. Release-Surface Lockstep

- Package metadata, shipped HTML version markers, release docs, and current-release readme/release-note pointers must move together to `v77.0.0`.
- `v76.0.0` assets and historical proofs remain for archive truth.

## Important Non-Changes

- The verifier proof-format and producer-payload contract remain unchanged.
- Trusted-signature success remains `signatureV4`-required for verified outcomes.
- Effective anchor context remains required for verified outcomes.
- Groth16 fields remain required and only real `g16:` proof payloads are accepted.
- The `v64.0.0` value-loop invariants remain in force.
- The `v70.0.0` public alpha law, `v74.0.0` Receiz Key baseline, `v75.0.0` local-continuity law, and `v76.0.0` World / Market / Public Proof law remain in force.

## Non-Regression Rule

A change is out of bounds for `v77.0.0` if it:

- routes Signal Circuit through fake loader shells or placeholder card truth
- downgrades sealed Signal Card art, claims, battle profiles, or lineage from older cached payloads
- treats pack opening as complete before sealed cards are issued, stored, and recoverable
- leaks private vault, pack purchase, admin, webhook, worker, or legacy keyring surfaces onto public Status
- mutates an original card claim when appending battle or progression records
- reuses `/make` as Creator Workspace truth instead of first-class `/creator`
- runs paid Creator production without idempotent wallet debit or explicit funding path
- restores Vault previews from anything other than restored bytes
- falls back to legacy V3 for old-Safari document sealing
- reintroduces automatic stale-runtime interstitials or stale-guard redirects
- replaces stronger local proof, profile canonical truth, or wallet settlement truth with weaker transport state
- reintroduces undefined release version drift between package metadata, docs, and shipped entrypoints
