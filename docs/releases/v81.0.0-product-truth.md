Release date: May 10, 2026
Status: Locked for `v81.0.0` release prep

## Purpose

This document defines the product-truth freeze for Receiz `v81.0.0`, the Signal Circuit Provenance Release. It carries forward the `v80.0.0` stable operating-platform law while locking the `v80.1.0` through `v80.3.0` stabilization line around Signal Card DNA, rewarded-card payment before reveal, wallet and calendar card activity, card transfer deduplication, discovery memory, mobile chrome stability, and release-surface coherence.

The product law does not change:

```txt
known verified truth moves first
later network work appends verified additions
visible surfaces do not rediscover or downgrade settled truth
```

`v81.0.0` applies that law to owned Signal Cards as inspectable Receiz assets whose origin, market movement, wallet activity, calendar history, transfers, arena appends, and proof context must remain additive around a stable original claim.

## Locked Product Truth

- Signal Cards remain Receiz assets, not disposable game state.
- Card DNA at `/game/cards/history` is an additive provenance surface; it may expose origin, holder context, listings, auctions, bids, sales, arena appends, and price points, but it must not mutate or downgrade the original sealed claim.
- Card pages, World cards, and share-card surfaces may link to share, card-world, DNA/history, and Find Cards routes without redefining ownership.
- Rewarded-card seal payment must verify the submitted claim before charge, spend eligible wallet lanes first, report exact shortfall, preserve compact idempotency, and emit wallet action-ledger activity.
- Wallet and calendar card activity must represent pack purchases, rewarded card purchases, rewarded-card seals, transfers, and receives as account history rather than hidden game-only state.
- Card receive activity must deduplicate repeated receive rows so one movement appears as one understandable ledger event.
- Card history and discovery memory may expand the same-session card universe after explicit user action, but must not silently redefine the first surface before the user acts.
- Mobile game chrome, top-bar popovers, white routes, card share animation, loaders, and rewarded-card stages must remain stable and inspectable without covering or replacing the asset.
- Signal Card art and proof-manifest layout may become richer only when proof, rarity, provenance, and readable facts remain visible.
- Release labels, service-worker defaults, offline verifier labels, README release pointer, release docs, and world resident worker markers all move together to `v81.0.0`.

## Out Of Bounds

A change is out of bounds for `v81.0.0` if it:

- weakens file-first verifier authority or changes verifier trust semantics in this repository.
- treats Signal Card DNA as a replacement for sealed card truth rather than append-only context.
- charges for a rewarded-card seal before validating the submitted claim.
- permits repeated rewarded-card seal attempts to double-charge or emit inconsistent purchase results.
- hides card purchases, seals, transfers, or receives from wallet and calendar history when those surfaces are meant to show account activity.
- allows duplicate receive activity rows to make card movement ambiguous.
- allows discovery memory or history hydration to silently redefine card state before explicit user action.
- lets mobile game chrome, popovers, route coloring, loaders, or stage sizing cover or destabilize card inspection.
- ships version drift between package metadata, release docs, README/release notes, static entrypoints, service-worker defaults, badges, or release-worker markers.
