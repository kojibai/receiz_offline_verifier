# Receiz v52.0.0

Release date: April 13, 2026

## Overview

`v52.0.0` is the market release where Receiz turns the market from a compelling surface into a governed execution system.

The visual shell matters in this release, but the real change is underneath it. The market is now defined by a canonical quote engine, bounded spread math, persisted quote evidence, partial share-certificate custody, dedicated market conformance, and lighter client/runtime paths so the product can stay premium without wasting work every frame.

The core Receiz rule does not change. The file remains the authority. Deterministic manifests remain the authority for route truth. Ledger-backed settlement remains the authority for payment truth. The market becomes an execution layer around those truths, not a replacement for them.

The product-truth freeze for this release is defined in `docs/releases/v52.0.0-product-truth.md`.

## What Defines v52.0.0

- Market execution now resolves through a deterministic quote engine instead of presenting spread capture as an extra visible fee.
- Buys and sells persist replayable quote evidence: reference mid, quote mid, bid, ask, spread, shift, and pressure inputs.
- Market pressure is bounded and explainable: inventory imbalance, recent flow, and volatility pressure shape the live quote without abandoning deterministic behavior.
- Share certificates now support partial balances, partial withdrawals, and partial redeposit flows instead of all-or-nothing movement.
- Market has its own conformance suite, badge, history endpoints, dashboard entry, and release-attestation coverage rather than hiding behind settlement.
- Market pills on feed, viewer, and live-player surfaces no longer depend on the full desk snapshot just to show one asset.
- The market UI keeps the darker premium treatment while the runtime beneath it does less wasteful rerender work.
- Release-governed version surfaces move together to `v52.0.0`.

## Since v51.0.0

### 1. The Market Now Has A Canonical Quote Model

Before this release, the market was already attractive and interactive, but its economic model still read too much like a fixed-spread execution helper.

`v52.0.0` turns that into an explicit quote system:

- a canonical quote model version now defines market execution behavior
- the model computes reference mid, quote mid, bid, ask, spread basis points, and quote shift basis points from deterministic inputs
- inventory pressure, recent buy/sell flow, and volatility pressure are bounded instead of ad hoc
- execution preview surfaces now resolve against those exact bid/ask quotes when available

That matters for three reasons:

- the platform can sustain itself through deterministic spread math rather than a tacked-on fee story
- user-facing execution can stay psychologically clean
- every market trade becomes much easier to audit and reason about later

### 2. Spread Capture Became Market Evidence Instead Of UI Friction

The important business change in this release is not “we added a fee.” It is the opposite.

Receiz market execution now treats spread as part of the quote itself:

- the execution price is the live bid or ask
- settlement occurs at that execution price
- quote evidence is written through trade metadata and formal snapshot columns
- the runtime stops layering a separate visible runtime fee on top of the trade

This is a better user experience because the market feels like a real market. It is also better operator governance because the system records the exact quote surface that produced the trade.

### 3. Market Persistence Became Replayable

Market economics only become serious when they are replayable.

`v52.0.0` adds formal quote snapshot persistence so every share trade can carry:

- `quote_model_version`
- `reference_mid_usd_cents`
- `quote_mid_usd_cents`
- `bid_price_usd_cents`
- `ask_price_usd_cents`
- `spread_bps`
- `shift_bps`
- `pressure_inputs`

That gives Receiz something it did not fully have before: one durable record that explains not only that a trade happened, but why that exact execution price was valid.

### 4. Share Certificates Became A Real Custody Primitive

Earlier market work made image-backed share withdrawal and redeposit possible. `v52.0.0` makes that behavior materially more complete.

Share certificates now support:

- withdrawing a partial quantity instead of forcing a full exit
- tracking remaining balance on the certificate itself
- redepositing all or part of the certificate balance back into the platform
- keeping market value and share quantity coherent across export/import paths

This is important because Receiz is not only trying to show ownership. It is trying to let ownership move while remaining legible.

### 5. Market Conformance Is Now First-Class

Settlement conformance was no longer enough.

The market now has its own standard surface:

- `/market/conformance`
- `/api/market/conformance`
- `/api/market/conformance/badge`
- `/api/market/conformance/history`
- `/api/market/conformance/history/rollups`

The market suite validates:

- quote determinism
- bounded spread and shift behavior
- hard-cap guard behavior
- execution-preview alignment
- persisted quote snapshot fields
- Vercel/Next trace inclusion for the required source files

This is the governance change that lets market graduate from “feature” to “system.”

### 6. The Premium Surface Now Costs Less Runtime

The market had also accumulated some avoidable client cost:

- the old ticker shape caused market surfaces to rerender on a countdown cadence even when they only needed pulse changes
- market pills were depending on the full desk snapshot to show one asset
- market bootstrap work was too serialized
- `/market` was vulnerable to a locale/timezone hydration mismatch

`v52.0.0` tightens that:

- pulse-only consumers now subscribe to pulse-only updates
- the full countdown path remains available only where a countdown is actually rendered
- the market pill uses an asset-scoped lightweight lane first and wakes the full market snapshot on demand
- server bootstrap work is parallelized more aggressively
- the market time formatter now renders deterministically

This is one of the most important “invisible” upgrades in the release. Premium interfaces only stay premium when they are quiet under load.

### 7. The Market Trust Story Now Reaches Public Surfaces

This release also extends market truth outward:

- conformance center now includes market alongside the existing standards
- status surfaces acknowledge market as its own governed suite
- powered-by / trust-row style surfaces now include market conformance where those badges are appropriate
- release attestation now includes the market conformance result

That makes the market legible not only inside the desk, but inside the broader Receiz trust perimeter.

## Since v50.0.0

`v50.0.0` made world a revenue operating system. `v51.0.0` made the public ladder honest about what the product had become. `v52.0.0` should be understood as the next obvious step:

- `50.0.0` built the live operator engine
- `51.0.0` aligned the product story to the real platform
- `52.0.0` gives the market its own canonical economic and governance model

That continuity matters because Receiz is not veering into something unrelated. The market is being layered into the same deterministic system model that already governs trust, world, settlement, and release truth.
