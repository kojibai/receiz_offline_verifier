Receiz `v100.0.0` — Proof Becomes Alive: Sealed Identity, Living Twins, and Operational Proof Rails

## Summary

`v100.0.0` is The Living Proof Release. It locks the complete 42-commit working set after annotated tag `v99.0.0`, ending at pre-lock candidate `e4780d5e7`, across 106 paths with 9,778 insertions and 245 deletions before the v100 design and release-lock records.

The release joins sealed Twin identity, deterministic living proof media, existing-object continuity, Profile and Market continuity, Sports proof recovery, and authenticated SDK application-state, custody, asset-admission, escrow, and event-proof rails. Proof objects can live, move, act, and compose while remaining deterministically identified, locally verifiable, custody-aware, and subordinate to no weaker session, database, SDK, MCP, or transport state.

Across the app and `@receiz/sdk`, durable proof memory remains **first admission only, then append forever**.

- App, SDK, MCP, runtime, service worker, public labels, offline verifier labels, and active release records move together to `100.0.0`.

## Product Law Preserved

- Sealed artifacts and proof-bound Twin identity remain stronger than atlas, UI, route, database, or session projection.
- Twin atlas backfill is a verified append beneath settled first paint.
- SDK and MCP remain convenience mechanics beneath proof identity, custody, ownership, settlement, and verification.

- Known verified truth paints immediately and later work may append only.
- Kai/Klok state remains the proof-object state machine; Chronos remains bridge and display time.

## Versioning Law From 100 Forward

- `100.0.Z` is a backward-compatible correction.
- `100.Y.0` is a backward-compatible capability or primitive extension.
- `X.0.0`, where `X > 100`, is reserved for an intentional breaking public contract or artifact boundary.
- Release size alone does not consume a major version. Named releases carry product magnitude.
- Runtime cache epochs and numeric payload schema versions remain separate from app/package release identity.
