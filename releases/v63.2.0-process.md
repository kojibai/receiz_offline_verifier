# Receiz v63.2.0 Release Process

Release date: April 19, 2026

## Goal

Make the current value-state and certificate economy official as `v63.2.0`: Reserve stays funded, Settlement stays proof-native, and share certificates become the visible bridge that turns priced ownership into Reserve after a real buyer or backed liquidity event funds it.

## What Worked

- The existing value-state foundation already separated settled balance from total/live value, so the release could add PHI surfacing without changing the base product for users who do not hold PHI.
- The market trade execution path already had enough deterministic truth to patch the visible wallet immediately after sell execution.
- The share certificate route already had custody, history, and trade primitives, so the public certificate page could become an ownership checkout without inventing a separate product.
- The existing secure checkout continuation frame already supported the right shortfall behavior: Receiz balance first, card funding only for the remaining amount.
- The service-worker navigation contract stayed stable while the release version moved to `63.2.0`.

## What Did Not Work

- The earlier provider-named settlement language was too implementation-facing for user liquidity surfaces. The product copy now frames this as secure checkout, settled value, card funding, and Sovereign PHI.
- The public certificate page previously asked users to sign in before the action. That added a second click and made the QR share surface feel less direct.
- Market sell balances previously needed a refresh to reflect the split between settled and Sovereign value, even though the trade result already knew the truth.
- The certificate chart front gave live value, but the back did not yet communicate the proof object clearly enough for a buyer.

## Practical Rule For Future Work

Whenever Receiz already knows the stronger deterministic value truth, patch the visible surface from that truth immediately and let background refresh confirm it. Do not make users refresh, do not show provider-specific liquidity language, and do not turn PHI into notes or external withdrawal unless a funded realization event makes it settled.
