# Receiz v63.2.0

Release date: April 19, 2026

Product-truth reference: `docs/releases/v63.2.0-product-truth.md`

## Title

Receiz `v63.2.0` - Sovereign PHI, Live Certificate Checkout, and Settled-Only Cash Instruments

## Product Truth

This release separates three things that must never be confused:

- Settled balance is cash-like value. It comes from funded deposits/payments or proceeds that were backed by settled value.
- Sovereign PHI is Receiz-native value. It can be sent and used inside Receiz, but it is not externally withdrawable by wire and cannot mint settlement notes until funded.
- Share certificates are the bridge for live/unsettled value. A certificate can be sold to a funded buyer or liquidity source, and that sale is the realization event.

The key rule is: notes stay clean as settled-value instruments; PHI/unsettled value moves through share certificates, not notes.

## What Changed


- Wallet settled value now counts explicit `settledUsdCents` metadata instead of treating every credit/debit as fully settled.
- Users with positive PHI/unsettled value now get the PHI/entitlement UI even if they were not originally entitlement-enabled.
- Wallet and Market show the non-settled lane as `Sovereign` with a `Φ` amount and USD equivalent, not as generic ecosystem money.
- Send and market-buy flows label mixed funding as settled dollars plus PHI.
- Wallet history now shows the same value receipt language on ledger activity: settled value, Sovereign PHI value, certificate shares when present, and funding source.
- Wallet settlement notes are blocked unless the requested amount is fully covered by settled balance.
- Direct market sells no longer fail just because the full sale is not backed by funded liquidity. The backed slice credits settled balance; the unbacked remainder credits PHI.
- Direct market sells now patch the visible settled and Sovereign balances immediately from the known trade result instead of waiting for a page refresh.
- Certificate owner liquidity sells now follow the same split: backed value settles, unbacked value returns as PHI.
- Market asset detail and trade preview now expose the liquidity truth before action: settles now, returns as Sovereign, and can certificate.
- Creator reserve certificates let an owner lock share capacity into a portable certificate without crediting themselves on mint.
- Creator reserve capacity is consumed when certificate shares are sold, preventing the same reserve shares from being minted twice.
- Public certificate pages now work as action-first ownership checkout pages: enter an amount, buy or sell, and Receiz ID starts only when needed.
- Public certificate pages now allow signed-in non-owner holders to sell their live shares from the shared certificate page.
- The certificate chart now flips to a live proof back showing certificate token, status, available shares, mark, value, cost basis, P/L, pulse, settlement rule, source, and verification links.
- User-facing funding language now says secure checkout, card funding, settled value, and Sovereign PHI. Provider names stay internal implementation detail.
- Release-governed version surfaces for the package and service worker line now move to `63.2.0`.

## Important Non-Changes

- Posting an asset does not automatically credit spendable wallet PHI.
- Minting a creator-reserve certificate does not credit the seller.
- Peer certificate sales do not magically increase platform direct-sell liquidity.
- Settlement notes do not carry PHI/unsettled value.
- External wire withdrawal remains downstream of settled value only.

## What To Remember

This release makes Receiz one coherent economy instead of two products. Settled dollars handle cash-like exits and notes. PHI handles Receiz-native value. Share certificates create the repeatable liquidity event that lets authors, holders, and buyers turn live value into funded settlement without creating unfunded platform obligations.
