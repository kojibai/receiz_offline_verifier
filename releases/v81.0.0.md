## Title

Receiz `v81.0.0` - Signal Circuit Provenance Release

## Summary

`v81.0.0` locks the first stable major release after `v80.0.0`.

`v80.0.0` made Receiz a complete proof, identity, value, creator, world, and Signal Circuit operating platform. `v81.0.0` takes the most user-visible system in that platform, Signal Circuit, and makes it feel finished end to end: cards are easier to discover, easier to inspect, easier to fund, easier to seal, easier to track, easier to transfer, and easier to understand as durable Receiz assets.

The release candidate is `23eec6d23`, 35 commits after tag `v80.0.0`, with 52 changed files, 8,289 insertions, and 385 deletions. This is not a label-only release. It folds the `v80.1.0` through `v80.3.0` stabilization line into one major lock for card DNA, rewarded-card payment, card activity, mobile stability, and release-surface coherence.

The product law does not change:

```txt
known verified truth moves first
later network work appends verified additions
visible surfaces do not rediscover or downgrade settled truth
```

`v81.0.0` applies that law to the part of Receiz where users now feel the most motion: owned Signal Cards moving through discovery, wallet activity, calendar history, transfers, marketplace events, arena appends, and proof inspection.

## What Is New

### Signal Card DNA

Receiz now has a dedicated Signal Card DNA surface at `/game/cards/history`.

This route turns a card from a beautiful asset into an inspectable provenance object. The new ledger path gathers card origin, current listing, auctions, bids, sales, verified arena appends, and ownership context into one readable history. It includes:

- a dynamic history route with canonical metadata and social images
- a server ledger loader for sales, auctions, bids, verified appends, owner profiles, metrics, and price points
- a card selector with rarity and type filters
- search across card title, owner, claim hash, listing state, market events, and append text
- price history charting for listing, auction, bid, and sale points
- event rows for sealed origin, sale settlement, auction opening, bid placement, and arena lineage
- focus URLs that preserve the selected card and claim hash
- progressive ledger hydration across additional card pages

This matters because Signal Cards are not disposable game state. They are Receiz assets. A card needs DNA: where it came from, what happened to it, what value it carried, and how it evolved without mutating the original claim.

### DNA Entry From Card Surfaces

Card pages, World cards, and share-card surfaces now expose explicit paths into Card DNA and card discovery.

The user does not need to guess where history lives. A card can lead to:

- its share page
- its card-world swipe view
- its DNA/history view
- the Find Cards experience

The release keeps the link model additive. DNA does not replace the sealed card page. Discovery does not redefine ownership. It gives the asset more verified context without weakening the original card truth.

### Rewarded Card Payment Before Reveal

Rewarded card sealing now has a real payment path before reveal.

The release adds `/api/game/signal-run/rewarded-card/seal`, backed by wallet balance checks, claim verification, an idempotent database charge function, insufficient-balance funding metadata, and wallet action-ledger emission. The seal price is fixed in the route at 99 USD cents, and the funding behavior mirrors Signal Pack purchase lanes:

- verify the submitted card claim before charge
- spend eligible wallet lanes first
- return the precise missing funding delta when balance is short
- respect Stripe minimum checkout behavior
- use compact idempotency so repeated seal attempts return the same purchase result
- append a wallet activity event for the sealed reward card

The user-facing outcome is simple: a rewarded card reveal is no longer a UI promise waiting for payment plumbing. It is funded, charged, idempotent, and visible in the product history.

### Wallet And Calendar Activity For Cards

Signal Cards now show up in the places where serious product activity belongs.

Wallet ledger activity now understands card transfer and card seal events. Calendar and wallet views can surface purchased packs, rewarded card purchases, rewarded-card seals, and card transfers as real activity rather than hidden game-only state.

This is an important product line. Cards live inside the value system. When a card is purchased, sealed, transferred, received, or tracked, it should appear in the same operational history a user already trusts for Receiz value and action.

### Card Transfer Deduplication

Card receive activity was patched so the same receive event does not show twice.

That sounds small, but in a proof product duplicate activity is not cosmetic. Duplicate receive rows make ownership feel ambiguous. `v81.0.0` treats the receive path as a ledger surface: one event, one movement, one understandable history.

### Card History Across All Cards

The history surface now reads across all discoverable cards rather than shrinking into a narrow local view.

The release also keeps manual "More cards" behavior in `/game/cards`, while adding same-session memory so returning to Card World keeps the user's expanded discovery context. This preserves explicit user intent: the app remembers the card universe the user intentionally opened, but it does not silently redefine the first surface before the user acts.

### Explorer And Discovery Fit

Explorer and discovery cards received substantial fit and density work:

- expanded vertical room in single-card explorer views
- larger proof manifest space on card backs
- better floating behavior inside popovers
- three-card bottom alignment for Find Cards from the share-card view
- safer fallback when entering Find Cards from a card page
- expanded buttons and improved top-bar popover treatment

This is the visible polish that turns a technically correct asset surface into a product that feels stable under real hands. More of the card, proof, and action space is readable without the surface feeling cramped or colliding with chrome.

### Mobile And PWA Chrome Stability

The release closes several interface regressions around mobile game chrome and route coloring:

- game bottom toolbar no longer leaks over top-bar popovers
- dark game routes no longer bleed styling into white routes
- game nav buttons are easier to hit
- card share animation no longer stops before transition completes
- popover loaders use a more premium liquid treatment without changing the route contract
- stage sizing for rewarded-card purchase and reveal fits the panel more cleanly

Receiz law is especially strict here: visible chrome may get richer, but it cannot create a second-load feeling or cover the thing the user is trying to inspect.

### Signal Card Art And Stage Polish

The Signal Run stage and card subject art received targeted rendering polish:

- card orb realism was increased
- artifact rendering around the orb was repaired
- outer rim treatment was improved
- rewarded-card stage sizing was tightened
- proof manifest layout on the card back now stretches into the available space between the description and actions

The product point is not ornament. Signal Cards are collectible Receiz assets; their visual system has to carry proof, rarity, and provenance without hiding the asset's readable facts.

### Contracts And Release Protection

`v81.0.0` expands contract coverage around the surfaces touched by the release:

- Signal Run route contracts now cover rewarded-card seal, payment-before-reveal, discovery, history, and stage behavior more deeply.
- World mobile bottom chrome gets explicit regression coverage.
- Market navigation and profile continuity contracts carry the release baseline forward.
- Explore packaging coverage reflects the expanded card discovery path.

The release does not ask tests to bless drift. The contracts encode the law that these surfaces must keep: stable mobile chrome, truthful card routes, explicit payment intent, and no downgraded card truth.

## Product Meaning

### Cards Became Inspectable Assets

Before this release line, Signal Cards were already sealed Receiz assets. `v81.0.0` makes them easier to read as assets.

A card can now carry:

- sealed origin
- current holder context
- market listing state
- auction state
- bid state
- sale history
- arena append lineage
- price points
- activity in Wallet and Calendar
- direct DNA navigation

That is what separates a proof-native asset from a collectible UI tile. The asset has a story, and the story is made of verified additions around a stable original claim.

### Payment Feels Native To The Game Loop

Rewarded-card seal payment now behaves like the rest of Receiz value:

- funded first
- idempotent
- wallet-aware
- action-ledger aware
- shortfall-aware
- schema-failure explicit

The user can move through the Signal Circuit flow without wondering whether the card, the wallet, or the database is the real authority. The answer is the same as everywhere in Receiz: verified card claim, wallet truth, and append-only ledger movement.

### Activity Is No Longer Hidden In The Game

Cards now show up in wallet and calendar activity because they are not just part of `/game`. They are part of the user's Receiz account history.

That is a major maturity signal. Receiz is converging product categories into one proof/value/account model instead of letting each route keep its own partial record of reality.

### The Surface Feels More Official

The release includes many small, visible fixes: buttons, popovers, height, route bleed, animation, card-back proof space, stage fit, orb rendering, and link recovery.

Taken together, they make the Signal Circuit feel less like a shipped subsystem and more like a premium product surface that belongs in the same application as Wallet, Market, Public Proof, World, and Creator OS.
