# Receiz v60.0.0

Release date: April 18, 2026

## Overview

`v60.0.0` is the major stable release that locks the full `v50.0.0` through `v58.0.0` buildout into one canonical product cut and rolls the complete repository delta after `v58.0.0` into the next governed release.

Since `v50.0.0`, Receiz has become a world revenue operating system, clarified the commercial ladder, turned market into a deterministic governed execution system, expanded public, Business, and admin surfaces, made continuity and fast-entry the default runtime behavior, moved share certificates into live custody, and restored authoritative market truth on first paint.

The post-`v58.0.0` delta carried by this release finishes the next stability layer:

- the homepage now shows live proof, live twin, and direct market action instead of static framing
- profile uploads and profile-original downloads are more authoritative, including canonical sealing and streamed-original recovery paths that keep verification intact
- forged twin portraits are minted as their own white-backed profile asset with explicit verify paths and an explicit apply-to-profile action
- share-certificate and market-wallet surfaces are tighter on compact devices
- production schema hardening now includes market conformance history RLS, auth init-plan policy repair, and high-value foreign-key indexes

There is no dedicated `v58.1.0` through `v59.99` public release note or tag in the repository. This `v60.0.0` note therefore rolls up the full repository delta after `v58.0.0`, including the absent `v59.x` interval and the current release-prep tree, into one coherent release record.

The product-truth freeze for this release is defined in `docs/releases/v60.0.0-product-truth.md`.

## What Defines v60.0.0

- Receiz now has one major stable release record that recaps the entire product arc from `v50.0.0` through `v60.0.0`, not just the latest patch set.
- The public front door is now live product: real proof playback, real twin preview, and direct market action are present in the hero instead of a marketing-only shell.
- Profile uploads and profile-original download routes now preserve or restore canonical Receiz verification more aggressively, including on-demand reseal and backfill paths for legacy streamed originals.
- Twin portrait generation now produces a distinct profile-ready artifact with its own verify path and an explicit user-controlled apply step rather than silently overwriting the public profile picture.
- Market and certificate surfaces keep the passkey, wallet, and custody story coherent on real devices, including compact wallet presentation and cleaner certificate framing.
- Release-governed version surfaces now move together to `v60.0.0` across package metadata, service worker defaults, public verifier artifacts, home, make, footer, legal, trust, plans, upgrade, and the active release docs.

## Since v58.0.0

### 1. The Front Door Became Live Product

The homepage no longer stops at explanation. It now boots from real proof and world data:

- the hero proof module loads from a real verified-player route payload
- the homepage now carries a live twin dock rather than a detached decorative shell
- the hero market lane now exposes direct buy action inside the proof experience
- home framing and hero proof are closer to the actual product boundary instead of an abstract teaser

This matters because the front door now demonstrates the product model directly: proof, identity, embodiment, and market are shown together instead of being described separately.

### 2. Original Delivery And Profile Uploads Became More Authoritative

Profile-original handling was tightened in two important places.

First, owner uploads now go through the canonical `/seal` document flow more consistently, including source uploads that previously behaved more like a streamed-original exception path.

Second, profile-original downloads now do more work to preserve verifiability:

- hash-verified streamed originals stay trusted when the original bytes already match
- legacy streamed originals can be resealed and backfilled on demand when the canonical artifact needs to be restored
- expected code and pulse continuity are checked before the fallback result is accepted
- the repaired artifact is re-verified before it is returned

The product rule stays the same: the file remains the authority. `v60.0.0` simply makes more of the legacy edge cases obey that rule instead of escaping it.

### 3. Twin Portraits Became A First-Class Output

World avatar generation now distinguishes between the live twin render and the profile-ready portrait:

- a dedicated white-backed profile image is rendered alongside the twin asset
- that portrait can mint its own Receiz original with verify path and verify URL
- the world forge now shows the portrait as a distinct result
- applying the portrait to the public profile picture is now explicit, reversible product intent rather than an automatic side effect of generation

This is a better model for both truth and control. The live twin remains the live twin. The profile picture becomes a deliberate publishing choice.

### 4. Market, Wallet, And Certificate Surfaces Were Refined For Real Devices

The runtime after `v58.0.0` also carried visible polish in product-critical surfaces:

- the market-page buy flow now falls back to the Stripe funding modal instead of dying on a missing wallet snapshot right after passkey sign-in
- the market wallet Receiz ID pill is centered and compact instead of drifting in the current layout
- certificate mobile presentation was tightened
- certificate footer language now says `CERTIFICATE CODE` instead of the weaker `NOTE CODE`

These are not abstract design tweaks. They remove real points of confusion in live money, custody, and sharing flows.

### 5. Production Hardening Moved Into Policy And Index Boundaries

This release also adds three production-facing schema controls beyond the `v58.0.0` baseline:

- `supabase/migrations/20260418001500_market_conformance_history_rls.sql`
- `supabase/migrations/20260418003000_auth_rls_initplan_policy_fix.sql`
- `supabase/migrations/20260418004500_high_value_fk_indexes.sql`

Together they tighten access policy around conformance history, repair auth init-plan policy behavior, and improve the database path around higher-value relational joins.

## Since v50.0.0

### 1. v50.0.0 Turned World Into A Revenue Operating System

`v50.0.0` is where Receiz stopped being only a proof runtime and became an operating system for live commercial motion:

- durable Contacts CRM
- world campaigns and concierge execution
- outreach, replies, bookings, and revenue attribution
- autopilot cadence and provider telemetry
- truthful rail-readiness and blocked-state handling

That release established the operator layer that everything later built on.

### 2. v51.0.0 Made The Commercial Ladder Honest

`v51.0.0` aligned the public product story to the system that already existed:

- Free, Authorship, Business, Automation, Enterprise
- cleaner upgrade and billing language
- consistent entitlement-facing copy
- Contacts and world operator hardening carried forward instead of being buried behind pricing work

It was the release where product framing caught up to product reality.

### 3. v52.0.0 And v52.5.0 Made Market Deterministic, Replayable, And Premium

The `52.x` line gave market its own governed system model:

- canonical quote engine
- bounded spread and shift math
- replayable quote evidence on executed trades
- partial share-certificate custody
- market conformance, history, and governance surfaces
- lighter value-pill and desk runtime behavior
- deeper public share routes and market-aware media flows

This is the point where market became infrastructure, not just presentation.

### 4. v53.0.0, v54.0.0, And v54.1.0 Expanded The Public System

The next block of releases broadened the platform around that market foundation:

- public market routes became indexable and machine-readable
- release attestation moved onto public-key verification
- Business became a clearer product front door
- admin became a real command deck
- signed-in workspaces moved toward one shared session bootstrap graph
- profile, artifact, and player surfaces got deterministic market preview before live hydration
- world loading and market-to-profile navigation stopped flashing weaker shells

This is where the system started to feel like one operating environment instead of several adjacent apps.

### 5. v55.0.0 And v55.1.0 Turned Continuity Into A Product Rule

The `55.x` line pushed continuity through profile, world, market, and player:

- guided owner setup inside `/profile`
- sealed avatar and cover media instead of decorative uploads
- freshest-avatar selection and preview locking across world surfaces
- stable market and player navigation
- passkey-first buy continuation
- exact-shortfall funding
- USD-authoritative server settlement for non-entitled wallets
- deterministic live-player fit and button-local route feedback

This is where already-known truth stopped being disposable after the click.

### 6. v56.0.0, v57.0.0, And v58.0.0 Locked The Runtime Boundary

The next releases turned continuity into a release-governed boundary:

- version surfaces were finally cut together
- malformed world cache state stopped breaking logged-in world entry
- fresh-account analytics drift degraded safely instead of breaking login
- profile, world, and market adopted fast-entry route warming more broadly
- share certificates became a native live custody surface with note-event ledger history
- public market inventory followed custody and visibility changes more quickly
- signed-in market entry became authoritative again on first paint
- wallet and chart-buy hydration were unified
- market transport became leaner and more deterministic

By `v58.0.0`, the runtime truth model was stronger and much more explicit.

### 7. v60.0.0 Closes The Gap Between Velocity And Release Truth

`v60.0.0` is the major stable cut that finishes this arc:

- the live product is now visible at the front door
- canonical proof behavior extends further into original upload and download recovery
- twin portraits are explicit, minted, and operator-controlled
- certificate and wallet surfaces are cleaner in the real compact-device path
- the production schema hardening keeps pace with the app layer

This release is less about changing direction and more about making the whole `v50.0.0` through `v60.0.0` line feel intentional, governable, and ready to stand as one product record.
